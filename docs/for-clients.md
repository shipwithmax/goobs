# For clients

You're working with an editor who is using Goobs to produce your videos. Here's what to expect.

## What you give them

- **A recording** of you talking about the topic. Phone camera is fine. Good lighting helps. A lapel mic helps a *lot*.
- **Optional context** — a brief, talking points, links, stats you want to mention.

## What you get back, in stages

Each stage is a real file you can watch or read. You don't need to know what "rough cut" means — just watch what they send you.

| Stage | What you'll see | What to look for |
|---|---|---|
| `2-assemble/rough.mp4` | A rough cut of you talking | Did they keep the right parts? Anything missing? |
| `3-enrich/enriched.mp4` | Same thing + graphics & charts | Are the visuals on-brand? Any factual fixes? |
| `4-polish/fine.mp4` | Cleaned up — better audio, smoother | Sign-off the content here |
| `5-render/masters/` | Final files for each platform | Pick which platforms you want to publish on |

## Giving notes

The most useful note format is **timestamp + what to change**. Example:

> 0:15 — drop this, I trip over my words
> 1:20 — the chart should show 2024, not 2023
> 2:45 — keep this, this is the best part

Your editor pastes these straight into the pipeline and re-runs the relevant stage. You don't pay for re-edits the way you do with traditional video — re-running a stage takes minutes, not hours.

## Why we use this

It's open-source, runs locally, and your footage doesn't leave the editor's machine unless you both decide it should. That's a real privacy story for sensitive material.
