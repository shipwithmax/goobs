# Editorial DNA: The "Goobs" Philosophy

This document contains the foundational editorial principles for this tool, directly answered by "Goobs" (a professional video editor). 

These rules form the soul of the AI prompts that drive the `assemble`, `enrich`, and `polish` stages. If a tool behavior contradicts these rules, the tool is wrong.

## 1. Pre-production ("The Pre-Pre-Production")
- **Core Truth:** The biggest problem is ignoring the "pre" pre-production.
- **Rule:** The majority of work should be done *before* the camera is set up.
- **Actionable Advice:** Require a polished script, pre-planned workarounds for roadblocks, a reasonable schedule, and conversations with post-partners to limit headaches later. "There is a reason the phrase 'we'll fix it in post' stings."

## 2. The Rough Cut ("The Radio Version")
- **Core Truth:** Good editing should build the structure first. An edit is never truly done; it just has to ship.
- **Rule:** Cut a "radio version" of the speaker's performance first. The goal is to make the core message as clear and seamless as possible via audio alone. 
- **Actionable Advice:** Once you have the structure, step away or sleep on it before reviewing. Subsequent edits are to refine "the structure into a livable home." When the story is told in the most concise and artful way, the cut is good enough.

## 3. Pacing & Visuals
- **Core Truth:** The speaker's words are the most important aspect of the video. 
- **Rule:** B-roll and cutaways exist *first* to cover up obvious gaps in visuals from the "radio cut", and *second* to texturally imprint an idea. 
- **Actionable Advice:** Only use a visual if it helps the viewer link and retain the message the speaker is delivering. Do not add visuals just to add visuals.

## 4. The Glitter Rule (Fixing Amateur Mistakes)
- **Core Truth:** Do not treat everything like a music video. 
- **Rule:** Never rely on transitions or generic B-roll "glitter" to carry the story. 
- **Actionable Advice:** Build the story completely barebones first. Only hang visual glitter on it *after* the core story works without it.

## 5. The Ultimate Philosophy: Invisible Editing
- **Core Truth:** "Story" is the most important word in video. If it’s a good polished story, it can be 1 take with no edits.
- **Rule:** Good editing is invisible. 
- **Actionable Advice:** The job of the edit is to move the story along. It should be *felt* and not *noticed*. If the viewer walks away thinking about how "cutty" the video was, the edit failed the story.
